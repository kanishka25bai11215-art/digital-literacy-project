Stay Safe Online – A Simple Checklist for Students

Let’s be real—most of us are online all the time, whether it’s for classes, payments, or just scrolling. So here are a few simple habits that can actually save you from getting scammed:

1-Don’t keep easy or repeated passwords
I know it’s tempting, but using the same password everywhere is risky. Try mixing things up a bit so one hack doesn’t mess up everything.

2-Turn on 2-step verification wherever you can
It might feel like an extra step, but it seriously makes your accounts much harder to break into.

3-Be careful on public Wi-Fi
College or café Wi-Fi isn’t always safe. Avoid logging into bank apps or anything important when you’re on it.

4-Don’t trust random links
If you get a weird message or link—even from someone you know—pause and check before clicking. It’s not worth the risk.

5-Think before posting personal stuff
Sharing too much online (phone number, location, ID cards) can make you an easy target.

6-UPI tip: Never share your PIN 
No legit service will ever ask for it. If someone does, it’s a scam—simple as that.

7-UPI tip: Double-check payment requests
A lot of people get tricked here. If you’re “receiving” money, you shouldn’t need to enter your PIN. Always read carefully.

8-Only download apps from official stores
Random links or APK files can carry malware. Stick to Play Store or App Store.

9-Log out if you use someone else’s device
It takes 2 seconds but saves you from a lot of trouble later.

10-Keep your apps updated
Those updates aren’t useless—they fix security issues too.

🚨 If something feels off or you get scammed:

Don’t wait or feel embarrassed—just report it quickly.

Go to the National Cyber Crime Portal
Or call the helpline: 1930

The faster you act, the better your chances of stopping the damage—especially for money-related scams.
